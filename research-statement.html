PhD Research Proposals

Generalizable 4D Gaussian Splatting Models

Going Beyond Per-Scene Optimization

Current 4DGS techniques require slow, per-scene optimization, limiting their practical application. This research aims to shatter that bottleneck by creating a generalizable, feed-forward model. The goal is to produce high-fidelity 4D reconstructions of dynamic scenes from monocular videos instantaneously, enabling real-time performance on new, unseen footage without retraining.

Research Plan:

1. Structured Feed-Forward Architecture: I will design a transformer-based network that decomposes the problem into predicting a canonical 3D Gaussian model of the object and a separate deformation field. This structured approach simplifies the learning task and improves generalizability.

2. Learning from In-the-Wild Videos: To train the model, I will leverage vast amounts of online video data. This requires a self-supervised approach that uses temporal consistency constraints, such as optical flow, to ensure smooth and realistic motion without relying on brittle SfM pre-processing for camera poses.

3. Synthetic Data Engine for Controlled Development: I will build a large-scale, procedurally generated 4D dataset, the "4D Objaverse." This controlled environment, with perfect ground truth, will be crucial for iterating on the core architecture and systematically addressing the sim-to-real transfer challenge.

Physics-Informed Deformable Gaussian Splatting

From Photorealism to Plausibility

While 4DGS can achieve photorealism, it lacks an understanding of the underlying physics, leading to visually plausible but physically impossible results. This research seeks to infuse these models with a physical understanding, enabling them to generate not just what a scene *looks* like, but what is physically *possible*.

Research Plan:

1. Bridge the Representation Mismatch: The core challenge is the mismatch between the rendering-focused 3DGS representation (a cloud of Gaussians) and physics engines that require surfaces. I will develop methods to derive a proxy surface mesh or density field from the Gaussians at each timestep, allowing for the application of differentiable physical penalties.

2. Incrementally Learn Physical Properties: The model will infer latent physical properties directly from video. I will start with simpler rigid body dynamics‚Äîinferring mass and friction‚Äîbefore tackling complex deformable objects and their properties like elasticity. Each Gaussian could be assigned a learned "physics code" to represent these properties.

3. Advance Towards Causal Reasoning: By developing a physics-regularized framework, this work aims to move beyond simple simulation and towards genuine scene understanding. Solving the representation mismatch is the key to unlocking forecasting and providing a foundational step toward true machine intelligence.
